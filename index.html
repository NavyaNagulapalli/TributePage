Sir Tim Berners-Lee

Inventor of the World Wide Web

Here's a timeline of key events in Tim Berners-Lee's life:

  1. June 8,1955: Tim Berners-Lee is born in London, England.
  2. 1973-1976: Berners-Lee studies at The Queen's College, Oxford, where he receives a degree in physics.
  3. 1976-1980: He works as a software engineer at Plessey Telecommunications in Poole, Dorset.
  4. 1980-1984: Berners-Lee joins CERN (European Organization for Nuclear Research) in Geneva, Switzerland, as a software engineer.
  5. 1989: Berners-Lee writes a proposal titled "Information Management: A Proposal," which outlines the concept of the World Wide Web. This lays the foundation for the modern internet.
  6. 1990: The first web page is created by Berners-Lee, and he developed the first web browser/editor called "WorldWideWeb."
  7. 1993: The World Wide Web becomes publicly available, and Berners-Lee establishes the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) to develop and maintain web standards.
  8. 1994: Berners-Lee starts working at the MIT Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory.
  9. 2001: He becomes a professor at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) and becomes a holder of the 3Com Founders Chair.
  10. 2009: Berners-Lee founds the World Wide Web Foundation, dedicated to advancing the open web as a public good and promoting internet access for all.
  11. 2012: He launches the "Web We Want" campaign to promote online rights and freedoms.
  12. 2013: Berners-Lee advocates for online privacy and the need for a "Magna Carta" for the internet.
  13. 2014: He receives the Turing Award (often referred to as the "Nobel Prize of Computing") for his invention of the World Wide Web.
  14. 2016: Berners-Lee is appointed to the Order of Merit by Queen Elizabeth II for his pioneering work.
  15. 2021: He auctions the original source code of the World Wide Web as a non-fungible token (NFT) for charity.
  16. 2023: Berners-Lee continues his advocacy for free and open internet, emphasizing the importance of data privacy and the ethical use of technology.

World Wide Web Foundation: Pioneering the Future of the Open Web

Sir Tim Berners-Lee's legacy extends beyond his invention of the World Wide Web. His vision of an open and inclusive internet, accessible to all, led to establishment of the World Wide Web Foundation in 2009. This organization has been a driving force in promoting the principles of a free, open, and accessible web for everyone.

Mission and Goals

At its core, the World Wide Web Foundation is committed to ensuring that the web remains a public good, a tool for empowerment, and a resource that benefits all humanity. The foundation's mission is to advance the open web as a force for good, focusing on key areas such as:

  • Universal Web Access: The foundation advocates for internet access as a basic right, striving to bridge the digital divide and empower marginalized communities with the tools to connect, learn, and thrive.
  • Web Neutrality: It champions the principles of net neutrality, advocating for an internet where all data is treated equally, regardless of its source or content.
  • Web Literacy: The foundation promotes digital skills and literacy to ensure that individuals have the knowledge and capabilities to navigate the online world effectively and responsibly.
For more information about the World Wide Web Foundation and its initiatives, please visit worldwidewebfoundation.

On the World Wide Web’s 30th birthday, i.e., on March 12, 2019, Sir Tim Berners-Lee reflects on how the web has changed our world and what we must do to build a better web that serves all of humanity.

"Against the backdrop of news stories about how the web is misused, it’s understandable that many people feel afraid and unsure if the web is really a force for good. But given how much the web has changed in the past 30 years, it would be defeatist and unimaginative to assume that the web as we know it can’t be changed for the better in the next 30. If we give up on building a better web now, then the web will not have failed us. We will have failed the web."
